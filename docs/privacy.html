Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 19, 2026

WindoM is a Chrome extension that replaces your new tab page with a personal productivity dashboard. This policy explains what data we collect, how we use it, and what stays on your device.

What data stays on your device

The following is stored only in your browser using chrome.storage and never sent to any server:

  • Dashboard settings and preferences
  • Todo items and quick links
  • Cached weather data and background images
  • Your display name and local customizations

Account (optional)

Creating an account is entirely optional. The dashboard works fully without signing in. If you choose to create an account, we store your email address and a hashed password on our servers, solely for the purpose of authentication.

Google Calendar integration (optional)

If you connect Google Calendar, WindoM requests read-only access to your calendar events. Your Google OAuth token is stored encrypted on our servers and is used only to fetch your upcoming events on your behalf. We do not read, store, or share your calendar content beyond displaying it to you on the dashboard.

Spotify integration (optional)

If you connect Spotify, WindoM requests access to your currently playing track. Your Spotify OAuth token is stored encrypted on our servers and is used only to fetch now-playing information. We do not access your library, playlists, or listening history.

Third-party APIs

WindoM makes requests to the following external services on your behalf:

  • Unsplash — to fetch background images
  • OpenWeatherMap — to fetch weather for your location
  • Quotable — to fetch a daily quote

These services have their own privacy policies. WindoM does not share any personal data with them.

Data sharing

We do not sell, rent, or share your personal data with any third party for marketing or advertising purposes. We do not use your data for any purpose other than providing the features of WindoM.

Data deletion

You can delete your account and all associated server-side data at any time from the Account tab in the extension settings. Local browser data can be cleared by removing the extension.

Security

All communication between the extension and our servers uses HTTPS. OAuth tokens are encrypted at rest using AES-GCM encryption. Authentication uses short-lived JWT access tokens and rotating refresh tokens stored as HttpOnly cookies.
